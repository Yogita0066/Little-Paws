Dobermann, or Doberman Pinscher

The Dobermann, or Doberman Pinscher in the U.S. and Canada, is a medium-large breed of domestic dog that was originally developed around 1890 by Louis Dobermann, a tax collector from Germany. The Dobermann has a long muzzle. It stands on its pads and is not usually heavy-footed.

Colors:
Black, White, Fawn, Black & Rust, Blue & Rust, Red & Rust, Fawn & Rust, Blue, Red
Life expectancy:
10 – 13 years
Temperament:
Intelligent, Energetic, Alert, Loyal, Fearless, Confident, Obedient
Weight:
Male: 40–45 kg
Female: 32–35 kg

Height:
Male: 66–72 cm
Female: 61–68 cm
